## MAE 3240 – Heat Transfer Problem Set #3

### Reflection
Problem Set 3 in MAE 3240 deepened my understanding of how the general heat equation is derived from fundamental principles and how it can be adapted to unconventional geometries. In Problem 1, deriving the governing equation for a thin rod with internal heat generation and lateral convection forced me to think  about energy balances within a given control volume. Setting boundary conditions reinforced how the mathematical formulation of the problem captures the physics of heat transfer. 

Problems 2 and 3 strengthened my ability to apply thermal resistance networks and steady state conduction analysis to practical engineering settings. The composite wall problem taught me how known temperatures can be used to find material properties such as thermal conductivity, which is directly relevant to troubleshooting and design work. The turbine blade problem connected heat transfer and mathematical concepts to a practical aerospace application where I was able to calculate why using thermal barrier coatings is so essential for keeping the turbine blades from failing. Together, these problems showed me that even using 1-D conduction models can provide great insight into material selection, and the importance of thermal protection in systems that are subjected to extreme conditions.
